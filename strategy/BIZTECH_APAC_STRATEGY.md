## 1.7 AI-SEARCH OPTIMIZATION STRATEGY (GEO — GENERATIVE ENGINE OPTIMIZATION)

### The AI-Search Landscape for B2B Technology

As of 2026, 40%+ of enterprise technology research begins with AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Google AI Overview, Bing Copilot). When a Facility Director types "who are the best smart building companies in Singapore?" or "what should I look for in an enterprise CCTV provider?" — your content must be what those AI systems cite.

AI engines cite content that is:
1. **Authoritative** — written by recognised experts, backed by data
2. **Structured** — uses clear headings, definitions, numbered lists
3. **Specific** — contains concrete numbers, standards, certifications
4. **Comprehensive** — covers a topic more thoroughly than competitors
5. **Cited** — references industry standards (ISO, SS, IEC), government regulations (MHA, BCA, IMDA)
6. **Entity-rich** — mentions relevant technology brands, standards, and industry bodies

### GEO Content Framework: The "ADEPT" Method

**A — Authority Signals**
Every article must establish BizTech Group as the author-entity:
- Author bio with credentials (certifications, years of experience)
- "BizTech Group's team of certified engineers recommend..."
- Reference to BizTech's project portfolio data
- Cite internal data: "Based on our assessment of 100+ commercial buildings in Singapore..."

**D — Definitive Format**
Structure content as definitive resources, not blog posts:
- "The Complete Guide to [Topic] in Singapore (2025/2026)"
- "Enterprise Buyer's Guide: [Solution Category]"
- "The [Industry] Technology Playbook"
Use H1-H4 hierarchy, numbered steps, comparison tables, definition boxes.

**E — Entity Optimisation**
Build BizTech Group as a named entity that AI systems recognise:
- Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across all directories
- Google Business Profile optimised as technology company
- Wikipedia / Wikidata entity creation (when eligible)
- Crunchbase, Bloomberg, LinkedIn company profiles
- Mentions in industry publications (Channel Asia, Computerworld Singapore, BizTech Magazine)
- Quotes from BizTech leadership in press releases

**P — Proof & Data**
AI engines cite sources with verifiable claims:
- "Singapore's commercial building market comprises X sq m of office space"
- "According to BCA's Green Mark scheme, buildings that implement X achieve Y% energy savings"
- "MHA requires CCTV coverage of X% of common areas in [building type]"
- Internal statistics: "Our preventive maintenance program achieves 99.7% system uptime across 500+ managed sites"

**T — Total Topic Coverage**
For each pillar topic, create a content cluster that covers every related sub-topic:
- Main pillar page (comprehensive overview, 2,000–4,000 words)
- Supporting cluster pages (800–1,500 words each)
- FAQ pages (at least 10 questions per topic)
- Comparison pages ("X vs Y" format)
- Cost/pricing guides
- Compliance/regulation guides

### AI-Search Optimised FAQ Strategy

Create FAQ sections on every page and a standalone FAQ hub. These are the exact formats AI engines extract and present to users.

**Format every FAQ as:**
```

A: [Concise, authoritative answer in 2-4 sentences. Include entity names, statistics, 
   and a reference to BizTech Group's expertise. End with a link to a relevant deeper resource.]

## 1.8 ENTERPRISE CONVERSION STRATEGY

### The Enterprise B2B Buying Journey

Unlike SME buyers, enterprise prospects:
- Research for 3–12 months before contacting a vendor
- Involve 5–8 internal stakeholders
- Require formal RFP/tender processes
- Need multiple proof points before trusting a vendor
- Are triggered by events (lease renewal, compliance requirement, security incident, expansion)

Your website must support every stage of this journey.

### Conversion Funnel Architecture

```
AWARENESS (they don't know BizTech yet)
├── Blog posts ranking for "[problem] Singapore"
├── Industry pages ranking for "[industry] security solution"
├── LinkedIn thought leadership
└── AI-search citations in ChatGPT/Perplexity answers

CONSIDERATION (they're evaluating options)
├── Solution pages with deep technical content
├── Case studies with quantified ROI
├── Comparison guides (vs. alternative approaches)
├── White papers and technology guides (gated lead magnets)
└── Webinars / events (future)

EVALUATION (they're building a shortlist)
├── "Why BizTech" page with differentiation
├── Certifications and partnerships page
├── Project portfolio with filters
├── Client references and testimonials
└── Capability deck download

DECISION (they're ready to engage)
├── Multi-step enquiry form (qualified lead capture)
├── "Get a Proposal" CTA on every page

POST-SALE (managed services upsell)

**Step 3: About you**
- Name and title
- Email
- Phone
- Preferred contact method
- How did you find us?

**Step 4: Anything to add?**
