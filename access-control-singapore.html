Access Control Questions Answered

An access control system manages who can enter specific areas of your building at specific times. Unlike traditional locks, electronic access control logs every entry event, allows instant privilege revocation when an employee leaves, and enforces complex time-based rules across dozens of doors from one platform. For multi-site or regulated organisations, access control is foundational.

Card-based systems use proximity or smart cards — simple, scalable, and cost-effective for most doors. Biometric systems (fingerprint, facial recognition, iris) eliminate card sharing and tailgating risks, making them preferred for high-security zones like server rooms and finance areas. Enterprise deployments typically use both — card access for general areas and biometrics for sensitive zones.
